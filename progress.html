I remember when I was about thirteen years old, I dabbled into HTML and inline CSS without even knowing it. It was a social media platform called, "Asiantown". Where members would create their own 'about me' pages. I remember seeing the HTML and CSS codes and would not understand it but knew that the codes would change the design of the page. I also remember having to search for codes to help me design my page. But the codes that I would use over and over, I remembered by heart. For example: <img src = " "> to input an image.

I remember I would change my page at least once a week because I loved being able to design it differently. I just had so many ideas and always wanted to make it look better. I would say that I did a pretty good job with designing my page, that random members who I didn't know, would reach out to me asking for me to design their page as well. I wasn't paid or anything, but I thouroughly just enjoyed designing pages so I always said yes. Looking back, I am surprised about how many people would trust me, a thirteen year old, to design their page for them.

Anyways, fast forward to now, it brings me back to the time when I was once as a kid ... designing my own page. A time where I would stay up until 2/3 in the morning on a school night just redesigning. See my progress within just a few months. I would say I've improved quite a bit. What do you think?
